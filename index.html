Books

October 5, 2016 · 1 minute read

Erfan My resources Aiden Julian Piracy of Digital Content Written by Piotr Stryszowski and Danny Scorpecci, This book was published on the 7th of July 2009 and was done as part of a project by OECD; a multinational organisation that focuses on economics and world trade, in order to give a greater understanding of digital piracy and raise awareness of the effects caused by intellectual property infringements. There are only 134 pages with 3 main chapters, a case study and 3 annexes, so it is fairly easy to read through and provides a good insight and overview of the issue of digital piracy.

Erfan Aiden Julian Can the madness be monetized? An exploratory survey of music piracy and acquisition behaviour Written by Storm Gloor and Clyde Philip Rolston, this 2010 article looks at digital piracy in the music industry and more specifically, why people pirate music and how it effects the industry. This is a good article as it presents some interesting ideas regarding the philosophy behind piracy, focusing more on why people do it, and suggests possible solutions/outcomes based on adapting to how people think instead of punishing them.
