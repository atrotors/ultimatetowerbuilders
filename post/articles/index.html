Can the madness be monetized? An exploratory survey of music piracy and acquisition behaviour Written by Storm Gloor and Clyde Philip Rolston, this 2010 article looks at digital piracy in the music industry and more specifically, why people pirate music and how it effects the industry. This is a good article as it presents some interesting ideas regarding the philosophy behind piracy, focusing more on why people do it, and suggests possible solutions/outcomes based on adapting to how people think instead of punishing them. The findings are based off a survey that asks “why non-payers acquired tracks without paying”, “Tracks downloaded per month”, and suggested pricing for music, among other things. The survey results are included in the article and provide information from several demographics. The article was part of the annual peer reviewed MEIEA (music and entertainment industry educators association) journal, which is made up of educators and academics in the music and entertainment industry.
