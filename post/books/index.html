Piracy of Digital Content Written by Piotr Stryszowski and Danny Scorpecci, This book was published on the 7th of July 2009 and was done as part of a project by OECD; a multinational organisation that focuses on economics and world trade, in order to give a greater understanding of digital piracy and raise awareness of the effects caused by intellectual property infringements. There are only 134 pages with 3 main chapters, a case study and 3 annexes, so it is fairly easy to read through and provides a good insight and overview of the issue of digital piracy. The first chapter looks at the different markets and industries that piracy effects (such as music, movies, software, books, etc.) and looks at how and why these industries suffer from piracy by looking at their economic properties. Chapter 2 looks at piracy as its own market, exploring factors like the supply/demand of digital piracy and what drives it. The third chapter discusses the actions industries have taken to try and combat digital piracy and the final chapter (case study), looks at the sports sector and how broadcasting, streaming, and dvd releases are effected by piracy.
